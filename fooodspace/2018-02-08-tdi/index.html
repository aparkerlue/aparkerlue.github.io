Revenue model

We proxy revenue using the product of the number of reviews a restaurant has received and the restaurant's average meal price for two. We fit a number of features including cuisine, location, and price tier on the revenue proxy, using an ensemble model that first applies ridge regression and then models the residuals using a random forest.

Our model achieves 80% precision and 97% recall for restaurants with above-median revenue proxy values, with an R-squared of 0.91.

Revenue-value of a rating star

While more stars in a review tend to suggest a superior dining experience, our revenue model suggests that the dollar-value of an additional star becomes meaningful only after three stars.

Pivoting on price

While sliding up the menu price scale generally correlates with higher revenue proxy value, the marginal impact of moving up a price tier varies considerably by cuisines served.

On- vs. off-avenue locations

On-avenue restaurant locations provide exposure to transit patterns that significantly differ from those of off-avenue locations. While avenues tend to be busier than streets, our revenue model suggests that off-avenue locations tend to demand a premium in many neighborhoods.

Restaurant recommender system

The recommender utility summarizes the output of a model that uses tensor-matrix factorization to decompose a three-dimensional array of average restaurant scores, indexed by cuisine, location, and menu price. This approach allows for both data compression and interpolation across missing values.

The model decomposes the original three-dimensional array into a small rank-3 tensor and three low-dimensional matrices. The restaurant score is a real number between 0 and 5 that is determined by the function \[ f(r, v; c) = r \left(1 - \frac{1}{cv + 1}\right), \] where \(r\) is the restaurant's average rating on a 0-to-5 scale, \(v\) is the number of reviews (or votes) a restaurant has received, and \(c\) is a parameter is calibrated to allow restaurants with a median number of reviews receive a score of \(f(r) = 0.5 r\).
